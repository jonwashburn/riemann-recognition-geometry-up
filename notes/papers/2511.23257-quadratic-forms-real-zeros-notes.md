### Quadratic Forms, Real Zeros and Echoes of the Spectral Action (arXiv:2511.23257v1)

- **Citation**: Alain Connes, Walter D. van Suijlekom, *Quadratic Forms, Real Zeros and Echoes of the Spectral Action*, `arXiv:2511.23257v1` (submitted 2025-11-28; published: *Commun. Math. Phys.* (2025) 406:312).
- **One-line relevance**: This paper supplies the key *mechanism* used in `arXiv:2511.22755`: **(self-adjoint, lower-bounded quadratic form + simple even ground state) ⇒ Fourier transform has only real zeros**, proven via a generalized Carathéodory–Fejér / Toeplitz argument plus Hurwitz.

#### What the paper actually proves (high-signal theorem shapes)
- **R1 (Toeplitz PSD rank‑\(n\) ⇒ unit-circle roots)**:
  - If \(T\in M_{n+1}(\mathbb C)\) is Hermitian, PSD, Toeplitz, \(\mathrm{rank}(T)=n\), and \(\xi\in\ker T\),
    then the polynomial \(P(z)=\sum_{j=0}^{n}\xi_j z^j\) has all zeros on the unit circle. (Corollary 1.1)
  - They give a C\(^*\)-algebra / operator-system proof (Step (1) in abstract).
- **R2 (continuous kernel analogue)**:
  - Replace Toeplitz matrices by convolution operators with continuous kernel \(h(x-y)\) on an interval, and replace \(P(z)\) by the Fourier transform of the eigenfunction corresponding to an extremal eigenvalue; conclude zeros lie on the real line (via a limiting/Hurwitz argument). (Steps (2)+(5) in abstract; see Section 3)
- **R3 (distributional quadratic forms → truncation matrices of “spectral action” type)**:
  - For real even distributions \(D\) on \([-L,L]\), they analyze the finite-dimensional truncations of the associated quadratic forms and show the resulting matrices have a specific structured form encountered in perturbative expansions of the spectral action. (Section 4)
- **R4 (generalized Carathéodory–Fejér for the structured truncation matrices)**:
  - They prove an analogue of Corollary 1.1 for these “spectral-action” structured matrices, extending the zero-localization result beyond classical Toeplitz. (Step (4) in abstract; Section 5)
- **R5 (main infinite-dimensional statement; real zeros of \(\widehat{\xi}\))**:
  - Let \(D\) be a real distribution on \([0,L]\), \(\widetilde D\) the associated even distribution on \([-L,L]\).
    If the quadratic form with kernel \(\widetilde D(x-y)\) defines a lower-bounded self-adjoint operator \(A\) on
    \(L^2([-\tfrac L2,\tfrac L2])\) whose lowest spectral value \(\lambda\) is simple/isolated with even eigenfunction \(\xi\),
    then all zeros of the entire function \(\widehat{\xi}(z)\) (Fourier transform of \(\xi\)) lie on the real line. (Theorem 1.2)

#### Two key “hard gates” (and why they matter)
- **G1 (simplicity is essential)**:
  - The paper emphasizes (already in the Toeplitz case) that **if the extremal eigenvalue is not simple**, the “all zeros on unit circle / real line” conclusion fails in general.
  - In fact they note a weaker salvage: *the intersection of the zero-sets of the eigenfunctions* is constrained (Remark 2.3), which they explicitly relate to the **radical of a quadratic form**.
  - **Why we care**: this directly mirrors the Section 8 “missing step M1” in `arXiv:2511.22755` (simple-even for the Weil ground state). If simplicity fails, the route must be adjusted (e.g. quotient-by-radical / common-zero formulation).

- **G2 (uniform-on-compacts / Hurwitz is the topology bridge)**:
  - Their continuous and infinite-dimensional arguments reduce to: build finite-dimensional approximants with real zeros, prove **uniform convergence on compacts**, then apply Hurwitz to pass “real zeros” to the limit.
  - **Why we care**: this is exactly the “Hurwitz gate” we packaged for Route‑3′.

#### Assumptions / caveats
- **A1 (simple isolated ground state)**: The real-zero conclusion hinges on the lowest eigenvalue being **simple + isolated** and eigenfunction **even**.
- **A2 (Hurwitz step needs uniform convergence on compacts)**: The final extension from finite truncations to the full operator uses Hurwitz’s theorem; so a core analytic requirement is uniform convergence of holomorphic approximants on compact sets (they provide this within their setup).

#### Extracted theorem statements worth remembering
- **Theorem 1.2 (informal statement in Introduction)**:
  - For a real distribution \(D\) on \([0,L]\) with even extension \(\widetilde D\) to \([-L,L]\), if the associated quadratic form with kernel \(\widetilde D(x-y)\) defines a **lower-bounded self-adjoint** operator \(A\) on \(L^2([-\tfrac L2,\tfrac L2])\) whose **minimum spectral value** is a **simple isolated eigenvalue** with **even eigenfunction** \(\xi\), then all zeros of the entire Fourier transform \(\widehat{\xi}(z)\) lie on \(\mathbb R\).
  - They warn this intro statement is “slightly imprecise” and point to their later precise version.

- **Theorem 3.1 (continuous kernel case)**:
  - For an even real continuous kernel \(h(x-y)\) on \([0,L]\), the associated compact self-adjoint convolution operator \(K\) on \(L^2([0,L])\) has the property that if the top eigenvalue is **simple**, then the Fourier transform of the corresponding eigenfunction has only real zeros.
  - Proof sketch: approximate \(h\) by step-functions → finite-rank Toeplitz operators → apply the Toeplitz real-zero theorem → pass to the limit via Hurwitz.

- **Theorem 5.6 (finite-dimensional “even case” with quotient-by-radical)**:
  - They build a *rank-one perturbation* \(D'\) of a reference operator \(D\) that becomes self-adjoint on the **separated quotient by the radical of \(Q\)**, then conclude a determinant polynomial has all real roots, and this implies the Fourier transform has only real zeros.
  - **Lean synergy**: this is exactly the same “quotient by radical then complete” move that we already use in `ExplicitFormula/HilbertRealization.lean`.

#### Mapping to our written + Lean proof

- **Route‑3 relevance (conceptual)**:
  - Offers a clean alternative gate: instead of proving the PSC splice identity, build a sequence of entire functions with zeros on the line (via self-adjointness) and prove they converge to `riemannXi` (Hurwitz ⇒ RH).

#### Verdict
- **Discharges a key sub-lemma** in the Connes/Consani/Moscovici “zeta spectral triple” strategy: the “real zeros from self-adjointness” part.
- **Does not by itself prove RH**: the remaining RH-hard step is still proving convergence of the approximants to \(\Xi\)/\(\zeta\).
