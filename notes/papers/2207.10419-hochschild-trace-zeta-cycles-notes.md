### Hochschild homology, trace map and ζ-cycles (arXiv:2207.10419v1)

- **Citation**: (Connes/Consani/Marcolli in the title page; arXiv metadata label) *Hochschild homology, trace map and ζ-cycles*, `arXiv:2207.10419v1` (submitted 2022-07-21).
- **One-line relevance**: Gives a cohomological/trace-map formulation of the ζ-cycle framework: identifies the “trace map” on (a quotient of) the Schwartz algebra with the classical map \(E\), and packages the ζ-cycles as a sheaf-theoretic object over the Scaling Site; this is structurally adjacent to Route‑3 operator programs but doesn’t obviously yield a direct `SesqMeasureIntegralIdentity`.

#### Results to extract (high-signal)
- **R1 (ζ-cycles and spectral realization statement)**:
  - Introduces ζ-cycles (circles of certain lengths with a Hilbert space containing the “ΣµE” subspace) and states a spectral realization: for a dense set of lengths \(L\), the spectrum of the scaling action on the orthogonal complement to the ζ-cycle subspace sits inside the imaginary parts of critical zeros. (Theorem 1.1, as stated in intro)
- **R2 (sheaf/cohomology realization over Scaling Site)**:
  - Builds a sheaf from the family of Hilbert spaces as \(L\) varies (Scaling Site parameter), then takes a quotient sheaf by the ζ-cycle subspaces, and claims the resulting \(H^0\) cohomology realizes critical zeros with multiplicity. (Theorem 1.2, as stated in intro)
- **R3 (Hochschild HH₀ identification + trace map = E)**:
  - Identifies \(HH_0\) of the adèle class space with coinvariants of the Schwartz algebra under \(\widehat{\mathbb Q}^\*\), and then identifies the invariant part with even Schwartz functions on \(\mathbb R\).
  - Key payload: identifies the trace map with the classical map \(E\) (Proposition 3.3 per intro).

#### Assumptions / caveats
- **A1**: A lot of the content is “framework-building” (Scaling Site, sheaves, Hochschild functoriality); extracting a direct analytic inequality or an explicit-formula splice identity is nontrivial.
- **A2**: The ζ-cycle spectral realization claims appear tied to the semilocal trace formula technology and its delicate principal value computations (intro around Section 2).
